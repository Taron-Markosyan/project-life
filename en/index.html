About Project LIFE

Project LIFE works to protect the rights of people with Type 1 diabetes who have been displaced from Nagorno-Karabakh, as well as local residents, and to help improve their health and social conditions.

Displacement from Nagorno-Karabakh has caused many people to lose their medical records and be cut off from treatment and disability benefits, while stress and uncertainty have made their health problems worse.

Our goals

  • Raise awareness — among people with Type 1 diabetes and the public alike — of health and social rights.
  • Help beneficiaries access treatment and necessary medication, and improve access to state support programs.
  • Create an informational and supportive environment where people with Type 1 diabetes can get useful information and professional guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Project LIFE, and who is it for
Project LIFE is a free, non-commercial initiative that supports people living with diabetes, particularly people with Type 1 diabetes and those displaced from Nagorno-Karabakh. The initiative provides information, legal and medical guidance, as well as digital tools — a Bread Unit Calculator and a Glucose Monitoring Diary.
How do I use the Bread Unit Calculator
The Bread Unit Calculator helps you work out the bread units in a food portion by selecting the food type and name, then entering the weight in grams.
What is the Glucose Monitoring Diary for
The Diary lets you record your blood glucose readings, track changes over time, and export the data as a PDF or Excel file to share with your doctor.
Are Project LIFE's services free
Yes — all of Project LIFE's services, including the information, the tools (Calculator, Diary), and legal or medical guidance, are free. The initiative is funded with the support of the European Union and never charges beneficiaries.
How can I get legal or medical guidance
Send your question through the site's Contact form, choosing either the "Lawyer" or "Doctor" category. Our specialist will review it, and you'll receive a reply by email — usually within 1–2 business days.
Does the information provided replace consulting a doctor or lawyer
No. Project LIFE provides general information and online guidance, but this does not replace an in-person appointment with a doctor, a diagnosis, or formal legal representation. For complex or urgent matters, we recommend seeking individual consultation with the relevant specialist.
