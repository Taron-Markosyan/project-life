Nutrition and Bread Units

Food has a direct effect on blood sugar levels, which makes understanding carbohydrates one of the most important skills in managing Type 1 diabetes.

What Are Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are found in bread products, grains, fruit, dairy, and sweets. During digestion, they're converted into glucose, raising blood sugar levels. There's no need to avoid carbohydrates, but it's important to know how much you're eating in order to calculate the right insulin dose.

What Is a Bread Unit

The bread unit (BU) is a widely used measurement in the region that makes it easier to estimate the carbohydrates in food. On this site, as is generally accepted, 1 bread unit ≈ 12 grams of carbohydrate. Knowing how many bread units are in a meal makes it possible to more accurately calculate the insulin needed, based on the ratio set by your doctor.

How to Calculate

Packaged food labels list the amount of carbohydrates per 100 grams. From that figure and the actual weight of the food, you can calculate the total carbohydrates in a meal. Using a scale and consistent measurements over time helps you more accurately predict how food will affect your blood sugar. To make the calculation easier, use the site's Bread Unit Calculator, which includes data for more than 100 foods.

Practical Tips

  • Favor whole-grain and high-fiber carbohydrates, which raise blood sugar more slowly
  • Spread carbohydrates evenly throughout the day, avoiding large amounts in a single meal
  • Watch out for hidden carbohydrates in sauces, drinks, and ready-made food
  • Use the Glucose Monitoring Diary to identify your own personal patterns between food and blood sugar levels
The information on this page is for general educational purposes. Set your individual carbohydrate targets and insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio together with your doctor or dietitian.
