Our Mission

Knowledge and Equal Access for Everyone

Project LIFE's core mission is to protect the rights of people living with diabetes, particularly among those displaced from Nagorno-Karabakh, and to help improve their health and social conditions. The initiative raises awareness of health, social, and legal rights, promoting equal access to treatment, medication, and state support programs.

Our Activities

The initiative runs a range of programs aimed at systematically addressing the challenges people face.

Educational and Awareness Events

Training sessions for school and college staff, as well as meetings with doctors and lawyers.

Social Media Campaign

Awareness videos and interactive content aimed at a broad audience.

Professional Consultation

Individual legal and health-related support from qualified specialists.

Community Building

A supportive community for people living with diabetes, both in-person and remote.

Emergency Medical ID Bracelets

Personalized bracelets with a beneficiary's vital medical information, for quick orientation in an emergency.

Digital Platform (Website)

A glucose diary, a bread unit calculator, and a contact form.

Expected Outcomes

  • Increased awareness among at least 1,000 people
  • Launch of a digital platform (website) as an ongoing source of information and guidance
  • Improved public attitudes through social media and awareness campaigns
  • Greater recognition of the rights of people living with diabetes
  • Engagement of young people with Type 1 diabetes as volunteers and awareness ambassadors
